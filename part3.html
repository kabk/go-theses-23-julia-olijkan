Part 3 
Our body is a canvas that we use to express our feelings, roots, experiences, beliefs. The way in which is strongly determined by culture and religion. In recent years, a new postmodern revival of extreme body modification has emerged, in which people allow themselves to be changed into new animal-like species through ear cropping, tongue splitting, subdermal implantation and uncanny prothesis.                                                                                                                                                 What is the attraction for animal-like creations and what manifestations are there? Thereby I am interested in which aesthetics and desire play a role. The animal is central to this subject not only because the physical form is equivalent to an animal but also wanting to become other than human on a mental level comes back to wanting to be another being.                                                                                                                                                 Among other things, I want to show the art of performing that touches on the view of this subject in different ways. My main interest lies in the phenomenon of body modification and its impact on people and nature. Does modifying your body mean that a new kind of nature will emerge. Do we see the physical change as a refuge and hope to let our physical world change with it.
